Nigerian Jollof Rice

Jollof Rice is a beloved West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, peppers, and spices. It is a staple at Nigerian parties and gatherings, often served with chicken, beef, or fried plantains.

Recipe Information

Preparation Time: 20 minutes

Cooking Time: 45 minutes

Servings: 6

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 6 large tomatoes (blended)
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 2 teaspoons thyme
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and seasoning cubes to taste

Instructions

  1. Blend tomatoes, bell peppers, and scotch bonnets into a smooth puree.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large pot, then saut√© onions until fragrant.
  3. Add tomato paste and fry for 2 to 3 minutes before adding the blended mixture.
  4. Cook the sauce until it reduces and the oil begins to separate.
  5. Add chicken stock, thyme, curry, bay leaves, and seasoning. Stir well.
  6. Wash the rice and add it to the pot, stirring until coated with the sauce.
  7. Reduce heat, cover tightly, and allow rice to steam until cooked.
  8. Fluff with a fork and serve with fried plantain, grilled chicken, or beef.

Tips & Notes

For smoky party-style Jollof Rice, allow the rice at the bottom of the pot to burn slightly for that signature flavor. You can also add butter for extra richness.
